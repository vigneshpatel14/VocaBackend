TL;DR


The AI-Powered Language Coach is a real-time, voice-interactive language learning platform serving schools (B2B), colleges, and exam aspirants. Students converse with a personalized AI agent (voice, persona, feedback tuned by age), receive detailed real-time feedback, and unlock a language-agnostic, CEFR-aligned curriculum in English, German, and French (with full expansion capability). The platform deeply adapts to four student segments: Primary School, Middle/High School, College/Undergraduate, and IELTS/Exam Aspirants—providing each with segment-optimized curriculum, assessment, agent persona, gamification, and reporting. AI powers all feedback, adaptivity, and analytics. Success hinges on deep classroom fit, measurable progress, and school subscription renewals.

User Stories


1. Primary School Student (Ages 6–11)


As a young learner, I want to play word games with a friendly AI animal, so I enjoy learning.
As a student, I want quick, colorful feedback and positive encouragement for every answer.
As a student, I want to show my parents the stars I earned and which words I learned.
As a teacher, I want to assign daily vocabulary packs and track word mastery for my whole class.

As a teen, I want to practice debates or discussions on school and world topics with an AI coach that “gets” my age.
As a student, I want honest, constructive feedback and clear tips to improve my fluency and expression.
As a teacher, I want to set homework, monitor progress over time, and prepare my students for state/board exams.


3. College/Undergrad Student (Ages 18–22)


As a college learner, I want roleplays and professional scenarios for interviews, presentations, and campus life.
As a teacher, I want to assign group speaking labs, monitor portfolio quality, and encourage real-world speaking skills.


4. Exam Aspirant (All Ages)


As an IELTS candidate, I want to simulate real exam parts and receive band-accurate, granular AI scoring.
As a coach/institute, I want mock test scheduling, batch progress, predictive analytics and reporting for cohorts.


5. School Administrator


As an admin, I want to add classes, teachers, students at scale and download ROI/usage reports for renewals.


6. Parent


As a parent, I want to see clear progress: time spent, new words, stars, and improvement each week.

User Experience


Student UX Flow (Core AI Conversation)


Student logs in (school SSO, parent invite, self-signup), selects assigned/chosen topic.
Meets age/persona-matched AI agent:
Primary (e.g. "Lingo" the owl): Greets with a rhyme or joke, speaks slowly, uses lots of encouragement.
Middle/High ("Alex"/"Max"/"Claire"): Friendly, guiding “cool” coach persona.
College ("Jordan”/”Lukas"/"Sophie"): Mature peer/mentor, offers professional tips.
Exam ("Coach Aria"/"ExamBot"): Formal, efficient, references exam rubrics, gives targeted feedback.
AI initiates interactive conversation (voice-first): prompts student with scenario, question, or challenge.
Student responds; AI listens, displays or speaks live targeted feedback:
Primary: “Try again! Can you say ‘yellow banana’ louder?” [stars fly out]
High/College: “Great word! But 'climate change has affect' — should be 'effects.' Let's try together.”
Exam: “You hesitated after 'On the other hand' — delay lowers fluency score. Try to link your points.”
AI adapts dialogue complexity in-session: scaffolds down if errors, increases challenge if fluent.
After session (5–10 min Primary, up to 30 min Exam), instant report card slides in:
Levels/Segments: Fluency, Pronunciation, Vocabulary, Grammar, Coherence, Task achievement
3 Common errors, 3 “Wow!” moments, urgent improvement tip, unlocked badge/XPs
Primary: animated sticker for collection; Exam: updated band prediction and actionable plan.
Teacher/Parent: dashboard shows all session data, summaries, comparative progress, with drilldown to mistakes, strengths, streaks.


Teacher UX Flow


Plug classes in via bulk import or manual entry; add students and assign language(s)
Use assignment builder to push topics and tasks per class/segment/language
See progress heatmap (per student/topic/skill), review flagged students, deep-dive session recordings or transcripts
Download/generate custom parent PDFs, export data for class analysis
Use “IELTS readiness meter” for exam segments


Parent/College Admin UX Flow


View student’s daily/weekly performance, game rewards
Notifications for progress, streaks, milestones, improvement

LMS Interoperability: Google Classroom, Teams, Canvas—REST API and SSO options

Exam-Specific Tracks (Per Language):


A. ENGLISH: IELTS Speaking



Part 1 (Interview): 30+ sample familiar topic Qs (work, hometown, study, food, weather, etc.)
Part 2 (Cue Card): 15 cue cards with 1-min prep, 2-min delivery.

E.g., “Describe an achievement you are proud of.”
Model responses (bands 5/6/7/8+)
Part 3 (Discussion): 20+ abstract/follow-up threads (e.g., “How can governments improve education?”)

Practice with follow-up Qs, challenge mode for higher bands


B. ENGLISH: TOEFL Speaking



Task 1 (Independent): 10 practice prompts (e.g., “What’s the most important animal in your country and why?”)
Tasks 2–4 (Integrated): 8 scenarios combining listening/reading and spoken summary or opinion.


C. GERMAN: TestDaF / Goethe-Zertifikat



Rollenspiel, Aussage, Diskussion tasks; topic packs mapped to official exam rubrics; model answers/feedback


D. FRENCH: DELF/DALF



Préparation à l’oral, sujet guidé, expression d’une opinion. Model questions, mini-bacs blancs (mock oral exams), analysis at each CEFR band.

Adaptive Learning Engine


At Registration: Adaptive placement test spanning receptive/productive (maps to CEFR, by language)
Every Session: AI recalibrates pace, task type, challenge level based on:

Score trends, mistake recurrence, speed of mastery
Surfaced “error memory” for 5 common errors (e.g., mispronounced ‘th’, wrong tense for past)
SRS (spaced repetition): resurfaces missed/forgotten words on optimal interval
Paths: Automatically routes into

Accelerated – unlocks higher-level tasks, debates, challenge badges
Standard – default progression
Remedial – triggers micro-lessons, foundational review, word games for stuck skills
Personalization: Encouragement and instructions tuned to confidence level (e.g. hesitant but correct speakers encouraged for speed/volume, over-confident with error get precision drills)
Teacher View: Adaptive “path” per student visible, assignment override allowed


Multi-Language, Language-Agnostic Content Architecture


Each language: complete set of themes, skills, grammar mapped to CEFR, but content (words, examples, agent persona/voice) is per language and per segment
Add new language: plug into category/topic/level shell, instantly fits into platform/school/teacher/analytics flow
Exam tracks: added per language, mapped to most common test (English/IELTS-TOEFL, German/TestDaF-Goethe, French/DELF-DALF)
Persona/Voice: Each language gets native synthetic voices, persona styling (“Renard” for French primary, etc.)
